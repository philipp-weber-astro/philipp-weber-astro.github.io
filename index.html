Research Highlights

Planet Formation in Dynamic Environments

My work focuses on how young stars and their disks evolve during phases of intense accretion, and how these environments shape the growth and migration of forming planets.

Numerical Simulations
Hydrodynamics of Debris Discs
Hydrodynamical simulations · FARGO-like codes · Dust Dynamics

I model the response of protoplanetary disks and debris discs to dynamical processes such as planet-disc interaction or insabilities. These simulations explore how outbursts modify temperature structures, trigger pressure bumps, and create conditions conducive to rapid planetesimal formation.

Observations
Imaging Planet Formation (?)
ALMA · Near-IR imaging · Disk substructures

Using ALMA and high–contrast imaging, I study rings, spirals, and asymmetries in disks around young stars. By combining observations with forward modeling, I constrain the presence of embedded planets and the efficiency of dust trapping.

Theory & Cosmochemistry
Linking Planet Formation to Meteorites
Chondrules · Disc transport · Planet-Disc interaction

I collaborate with cosmochemists to relate the dynamical histories in simulations to meteoritic constraints. This allows us to test whether dynamic separation and mixing can account for observed chondrule and CAI properties.

Science Communication

Sharing the Story of Planet Formation

I am committed to making astronomy accessible, from public talks and school workshops to multimedia projects that blend science with art and storytelling.

Public Talk Organizer
“Space is the Place” Lecture Series

I founded the public lectures series Space is the Place, that blends astronomical outreach with Jazz concerts, tailored to non–expert audiences. These events take place in Santiago night life, in bars, theatres, cultural centres and musical festivals. They focus on storytelling, highlighting both recent discoveries and the human side of research.

Workshops & Schools
Organizing scientific interaction

I co-organized the first FARGO3D workshop in January 2024, a week-long event to teach students and researchers how to use and develop the FARGO3D hydrodynamics code for protoplanetary disk simulations. I also co-organized the international workshop on planet formation around outbursting stars, suitingly called "Born in Fire".
